Quantum at Maryland

UMD is a hub for quantum research and development. Here, over 200 quantum scientists and engineers are exploiting the peculiarities of quantum physics to usher in a new age of technology: quantum computers capable of currently impossible calculations, ultra-secure quantum networking and exotic new quantum materials.

Propelled by research partnerships with the National Institute of Standards and Technology, UMD is home to an expansive quantum research enterprise. UMD’s quantum portfolio includes world-class research institutes, international collaborations, government partnerships and industry connections.

The Quantum Promise

UMD's quantum research combines physics, computer science, engineering and materials science, resulting in applications with the power to change the world. Maryland stands at the forefront in a range of vital technologies of the future:

Computing

Quantum computers leverage counterintuitive features of quantum physics to execute certain tasks faster than current supercomputers.

Crytography

Quantum technology could enable new security measures for everything from communication to personal health records to national secrets.

Materials

Completely new exotic materials may improve current devices and open the door to technologies not yet envisioned.

Networks

Scientists are building optical networks to send and receive quantum information over distances large and small.

Machine Learning

Quantum physics could supercharge traditional machine learning. On the flip side, machine learning might help scientists understand quantum phenomena.

Systems Research

Developing advanced quantum systems can open up new avenues of discovery and spark unexpected applications.

Sensors

Quantum systems have an inherent instability that can be harnessed to detect subtle environmental changes, leading to better measurement tools.

Software

Scientists are developing quantum-specific programs that will run on quantum computers and searching for new applications for these revolutionary machines.

Centers and Institutions

Joint Quantum Institute

The Joint Quantum Institute (JQI), founded in 2006, is the cornerstone of UMD’s quantum enterprise—internationally renowned as a preeminent center for groundbreaking research and innovative ideas. A research partnership with NIST supported by the Laboratory for Physical Sciences, JQI is dedicated to the broad study of quantum science, from theory to experiment, on a host of platforms.

Explore JQI

Joint Center for Quantum Information and Computer Science

The Joint Center for Quantum Information and Computer Science (QuICS) is a collaboration with NIST that expands research at the junction of quantum physics, computer science and information theory, enabling the full potential of quantum computing.

Explore Quics

Quantum Technology Center

The Quantum Technology Center joins researchers in engineering and physics to focus on translating quantum physics into innovative technologies.

Explore QTC

Condensed Matter Theory Center

The Condensed Matter Theory Center, partially supported by Microsoft Research and led by Distinguished University Professor Sankar Das Sarma, has made pioneering contributions to exotic approaches to quantum computing now being pursued worldwide.

Explore CMCT

Center for Quantum Materials and Devices

The Center for Quantum Materials and Devices is a specialized research center in the Department of Physics where scientists explore existing superconductors, as well as the study and creation of novel quantum materials, with the goal of enabling new devices, ranging from sensors to secure communications equipment.

Explore CNAM

Maryland Quantum Alliance

The University of Maryland is convening the Maryland Quantum Alliance, a collaborative consortium of research institutions from throughout the region. The alliance will further enable quantum researchers on campus to deepen and expand their partnerships.

The Maryland Quantum Alliance will:

  • Organize workshops and provide networking opportunities
  • Fast-track workforce development through interdisciplinary quantum education
  • Explore opportunities for industry engagement through rotation programs and internships
